Google (and others) stopped indexing sites automatically

June 29, 2023

Intro

This all started with with me stumbling upon an article in hackernews about google not indexing sites that are not are not added to google manually. This seemed quite odd since I remember that it took at most 3-4 days for google to pickup any sites in the past. After discussion with a couple of my friends who actually do this for a living they convinced me that the guy was just wrong and google works as expected. So I decided to do some testing and disprove that guy from original article.

At the same time I was doing research on Swing VPN and was planning to write a short article about it. So it was a good opportunity to test the hypothesis. My plan was to write an article and share with some of my friends who already had blogs indexed by google. So in a week a google with either index it [or not].

The thing is that the article became slightly popular and it was on top page of hackernews, it was on reddit and more that a few dozen articles linked to it. So I there is no way for google or any other search engine miss it. So google and other search engines definitely have seen it.

I used a absolutely new site ’lecromee.github.io’ with a specifically nonexistent word ’lecromee’ so that it make it easier for us to see the results later. My preferred analytics tool is ‘google analytics’ but for the project I decided to use something else just for testing.

Testing init

So let’s start with simple google test. The simplest way to do it is to ask google to search pages from a website with ‘site:’ prefix in the search query. The search was redone on 28th of June to verify results.

Since I am not an expert on search and SEO I thought maybe google has some issues with subdomains or maybe subdomains of github.io. So let’s try to find if google has issues with indexing them. If we change our query from ’lecromee.github.io’ to ‘*.lecromee.github.io’ we should get a list of some site and we do.

Some of the sites redirect to another host so let’s just focus on the ones that are actually static. If you weed out all the sites that redirect to other domains and just leave the ones that point to a static site at github.io then you can see that all of them either use google analytics, google tag manager a google-site-verification meta tag. I couldn’t find a single site that was google searchable but didn’t have a some google product embedded in it.

Maybe google couldn’t find the content and just missed it? Let try to search for the title of the article that was the most popular. I did a search for “Swing VPN app is a DDOS botnet” with quotes just to make sure it will search for exactly match. If found not too much. 7 pages of of different sites that linked directly to this article. Which leads us to conclusion that the search engine indeed found the link while parsing those sites and probably went to check my site but never added it to the index. I probably should have used my own server so that I later I could have dug into the logs and verified that bot actually visited but I didn’t and there is very low change of writing a another popular article fot this test so let’s work with what we got.

Other engines

So my next natural action was to do the same search in other search engines. I started with bing and bing returned a single result. It looked to me that github belongs to microsoft as is bing, so maybe they treat that site differently and at least have a single link to it. Because even though bing does find when you directly ask for a specific site it does not has any information about pages and the content on the site. Other search engines ddg.com and yahoo.com at the beginning didn’t have any results when I searched for ‘site:lecromee.github.io’ but later after some time started returning a single ‘redirect’ link the same as bing. Maybe they share information or maybe they use bing under the hood or maybe they just use similar algorithm.

Yandex.com even now behaves the same as google and does not return any result for a ‘site:lecromee.github.io’ search. I don’t know the reason behind all this but maybe non of the search engines do automatic indexing anymore?

Then I repeated the same process of searching for “Swing VPN app is a DDOS botnet” title of the article and went through all search pages and tried to find a link to my static site and none of them were indexing it even though they indexed all the articles that mentioned it.

This is very surprising as I know what to do to get into google. Just put their tracker on the site and it is done. Yandex also has some kind of trackers which should help. But what about others? <joke>Do they just copy from google or maybe when they they crawl websites and don’t find any trackers on it they just ignore it?</joke>.

Sidenote

I myself use google analytics for all myself used google analytics for all my other projects and I like it and never had issues with indexing. For this project I used https://matomo.org and it is really fun to use. It feels like it give more accurate information and real time feature is really good to visualize dynamics in the current moment. I highly suggest to try it.

Conclusion

From this quick experiment I found that google and other search engines just don’t bother indexing your site even thought there are more than 100 sites back linking to my site. It could be that new algorithms require to have trackers to verify you through them, security reason, malicious business practices, or any other reason. Well no matter what if you want to have your site indexed, just uploading it online won’t do it. At least put some trackers from google it will definitely help get into the indexing process.
